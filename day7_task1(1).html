Chapter 1: Actual name and identity

Allu Arjun is an Indian actor who works in Telugu cinema and he is known to be one of the highest-paid actors.
His screen name is became as Stylish Star Allu Arjun, including fans call him as Bunny.

Chapter 2: Family and DOB

Allu Arjun was born on 8 April 1982 in a Telugu family to film producer Allu Aravind and Nirmala in Madras (present Chennai).
His paternal grandfather was the noted film comedian Allu Ramalingaiah who appeared in over 1000 films.
Their native place is Palakollu of West Godavari district, Andhra Pradesh.
Allu Arjun grew up in Chennai before their family moved to Hyderabad in his 20s.
He is the second of three children. His elder brother Venkatesh is a businessman while his younger brother Sirish is also an actor.
His paternal aunt is Surekha Konidela, the wife of actor Chiranjeevi. He is the first cousin of actor Ram Charan.

Chapter 3: Career beginning stage

After playing as a child artist in Vijetha (1985) and as a dancer in Daddy (2001), he made his adult debut in Gangotri(2003).
The film Gangotri was directed by K. Raghavendra Rao with his father Allu Aravind producing, along with C. Ashwini Dutt.
Praising his acting performance, Jeevi of Idlebrain criticised his looks in the film and added that he should choose roles to amplify strengths and nullify weaknesses.
He then appeared in Sukumar's Arya(2004). He plays the eponymous lead, an outgoing and free-spirited boy.
The film proved to be his breakthrough, earning a first nomination for the Filmfare Award for Best Actor - Telugu.
The film was a critical and commercial success, grossing over ₹30 crore, with a production budget of ₹4 crore.
In 2006, the film was dubbed and released in Malayalam in Kerala.
Owing to the film's success, he received major acclaim across the region and the Malayali people.

Chapter 4: Movies which made successful hero

Allu Arjun rose to prominence starring in Sukumar's cult classic Arya (2004).
He consolidated his reputation with the action films Bunny (2005) and Desamuduru (2007).
In 2008, he starred in the romantic drama movie called Parugu.
Allu Arjun went on to star in notable films as below:
Arya 2 (2009), Vedam (2010), Julayi (2012), Race Gurram (2014), S/O Satyamurthy (2015), Rudhramadevi (2015), Sarrainodu (2016), DJ: Duvvada Jagannadham (2017) and Ala Vaikunthapurramuloo (2020).
He received high acclaim for his performance in Pushpa: The Rise(2021), which emerged as the highest-grossing Indian film in 2021 and ranks among the highest-grossing Telugu films of all time.
The film also earned him his first National Film Award for Best Actor, and fourth Filmfare Award for Best Actor - Telugu.

Chapter 5: List of received Awards

For the movie Arya(2004), he earned a Nandi Special Jury Award and CineMAA Award for Best Actor (Critics).
For the movie Parugu(2008), he won his first Filmfare Award for Best Actor - Telugu.
His performances as a lower-class cable operator in Vedam(2010) and a carefree street smart man in Race Gurram(2014) made Allu Arjun to win two Filmfare Awards for Best Actor - Telugu.
He also won the Filmfare Award for Best Supporting Actor for his portrayal of prince Gona Ganna Reddy in Rudhramadevi(2015).
Pushpa: The Rise(2021) film also earned him his first National Film Award for Best Actor, and fourth Filmfare Award for Best Actor.
